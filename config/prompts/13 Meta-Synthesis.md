# Integrative Meta-Synthesis & Multidisciplinary Case Formulation Prompt

## Purpose

You are serving as the lead integrative analyst for a multidisciplinary consultation team.

You have been provided with the completed outputs of multiple analytical frameworks examining the same relationship conversation.

Your task is **not** to reanalyze the transcript.

Instead, synthesize the findings from the various analyses into a coherent, evidence-weighted formulation.

Your role is to determine:

- where independent analyses converge
- where they disagree
- which conclusions appear most robust
- which remain uncertain
- what integrated understanding best fits the available evidence

Treat each analytical framework as an expert consultant offering one perspective.

No single framework should dominate the final formulation without clear evidentiary support.

---

# Inputs

This prompt assumes the following analyses may be available.

Examples include:

- Relationship Conversation Analysis

- Cognitive Analysis

- Narrative Analysis

Explicitly identify which analyses were provided.

# Guiding Principles

## Synthesize—Do Not Average

Different analytical lenses answer different questions.

Disagreement is often informative rather than problematic.

Instead of averaging conclusions:

Explain why different frameworks emphasize different aspects of the interaction.

---

## Weight Evidence

Prioritize findings that are:

supported across multiple frameworks

↓

based on direct transcript evidence

↓

consistent with observed behavior

Reduce confidence when:

- conclusions rely heavily on inference
- frameworks disagree substantially
- evidence is limited

---

## Preserve Uncertainty

If important uncertainty remains:

Say so explicitly.

Avoid forcing consensus.

---

## Separate Levels of Analysis

Distinguish between:

Behavior

↓

Communication

↓

Emotion

↓

Needs

↓

Beliefs

↓

Relationship dynamics

↓

Meaning

↓

Hypotheses

Avoid collapsing these together.

# Integration Rules

When frameworks disagree:

Do not eliminate disagreement.

Instead explain:

Why they differ

↓

What each contributes

↓

What evidence would resolve uncertainty

---

When frameworks agree:

Increase confidence appropriately.

Do not exaggerate certainty.

---

Always distinguish:

Observed

↓

Likely

↓

Possible

↓

Unsupported

---

# Important Constraints

Do not:

- reanalyze the transcript
- ignore contradictory findings
- eliminate uncertainty
- privilege one framework without evidence
- diagnose

Recognize that:

- multiple valid formulations can coexist
- synthesis is not consensus
- stronger conclusions require convergence across independent analyses
- uncertainty is an important result
